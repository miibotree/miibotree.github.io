What does my CDMA work for my smartphone?

发表于 2014-03-31

Recently my Nokia is broken, so I bought an iPhone 5S and is now using 4G.

As a student of communication engineering, It’s a great need to know the principles of 3G/4G.

Here I learned a course in coursera :Networks: Friends, Money, and Bytes.

The course answered 20 Questions about networks using math as a tool.

https://class.coursera.org/friendsmoneybytes-004/lecture

below is what I’ve learned about the first problem: What does my CDMA work for my smartphone?

 

1.the technology used in the 3G.

3G use CDMA (码分多址 code division multiple access). And here is a wikipedia page introduce CDMA:

http://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%A0%81%E5%88%86%E5%A4%9A%E5%9D%80

 

we should know the difference between CDMA, TDMA, FDMA, and also OFDM used in 4G.

 

2.cellular network

notice that BS means Base Station, and MS means Mobile Station(our iPhone, Android devices)

The structure is cellular because the signal is transmitted by Electromagnetic(电磁波), so signal will be attenuated by obstacles. So there are many MS in the cellular network.

Here we can see: technology must depend on the physical.

 

3.near far problem

however, in a cellular, there may be lots of MS, some is near the BS while others is far. Thus a problem occur, We call the near far problem : signals BS received from the near MS is much more powerful than the far.Thus the far MS may not communicate with BS because the signal is covered by the near MS’s signal. Let’s see a picture:

 

G11 and G22(generally Gii) are the direct channel gains, and G12 and G21 are interference(干扰) channel gains. If G12 is stronger than G11, here the problem occur.

So the main technology we have to solve is the near far problem: by control the power.

Means every MS control it’s power(far more and near less).so when the signals reach to BS, they almost the same.

PS:this model is very similar to the mutual information model in Theory And Coding.

4.DPC(discrete power control algorithm)

Gij is channel gains, pi is MSi’s power and ni is noise

so SIR is ratio of signal/(interference + noise)

 

the signal is discrete, and here we give the DPC algorithm:

 

 

 

ri is the targer SIR, and SIRi[t] is a feedback(反馈)from the BS. the algorithm is very simple because there is no parameters. and it’s both elegant and practical.
